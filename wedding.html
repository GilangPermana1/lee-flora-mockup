Why Singapore Brides Are Choosing Preserved & Combo Flowers

Flowers That Last Beyond Your Wedding Day

More Singapore brides are choosing preserved and artificial flowers over fresh for their weddings. Preserved flowers are real flowers treated to last 2–5 years, meaning your bridal bouquet becomes a permanent keepsake. When blended with premium artificial greenery, the result is a fuller, more dynamic arrangement at a fraction of the cost of all-fresh flowers.

At Lee Flora, we supply all three types — preserved, dried, and artificial — so you can mix and match to create your perfect wedding look at wholesale prices.

Wedding Flower Questions

Wedding Flower Questions

Can Lee Flora make full bouquets or do wedding arrangements?
Lee Flora is a wholesale supplier — we sell the flowers and materials. For full professional arrangements, we can refer you to florists in our directory who source from us. Alternatively, we'll teach you how to make arrangements yourself.
Can I use preserved and artificial flowers for my wedding?
Absolutely — many Singapore brides now use combo arrangements. Preserved roses in a bouquet, faux greenery for volume, and dried pampas for texture creates a stunning, cost-effective arrangement that looks incredible in photos.
What is a preserved flower hantaran cost?
DIY preserved flower hantarans using wholesale materials from Lee Flora typically cost $30–$80 per tray depending on the flowers selected. Our team can advise on the best flowers for Malay and Chinese wedding traditions.
How much does a DIY preserved bridal bouquet cost?
Using wholesale materials from Lee Flora, a DIY preserved bridal bouquet typically costs $60–$150 in materials, compared to $200–$500+ for a professionally made fresh bouquet that wilts after the day.
Do I need experience to DIY my own wedding flowers?
No experience needed. Our team at the showroom will guide you through technique, colour selection, and assembly. Many brides who have never arranged flowers before create stunning bouquets with our guidance.
